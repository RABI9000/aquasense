1. INTRODUCTION: WHAT IS SAR & WHY IS IT USED?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sentinel-1 is a polar-orbiting satellite mission carrying a C-band Synthetic
Aperture Radar (SAR) sensor. Unlike optical satellites (like Sentinel-2 or 
Landsat) which cannot see through clouds or operate at night, SAR is an active 
sensor. It transmits microwave pulses (C-band, ~5.4 GHz) and measures the intensity
of the reflected echo (backscatter, denoted as sigma-zero, σ0).

SAR backscatter is highly sensitive to:
A. DIELECTRIC PROPERTIES: Water has a high dielectric constant (~80) compared
   to dry soil (~3-5). High soil moisture dramatically increases the soil's
   dielectric constant, causing stronger radar reflections (higher backscatter).
B. SURFACE ROUGHNESS: Rougher soils scatter waves in all directions, increasing
   backscatter.
C. CANOPY VOLUME SCATTERING: Plant vegetation leaves, stems, and stalks scatter
   microwaves, attenuating the signal before it reaches the soil.

To isolate pure soil moisture, our pipeline processes raw co-polarized (VV) and
cross-polarized (VH) radar channels through a strict, multi-phase pipeline.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. DETAILED CORE PIPELINE PHASES & PYTHON CODE MATCH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

================================================================================
PHASE 1: DATA ACQUISITION & GEE EXTRACTION
================================================================================
The raw SAR satellite data is accessed via the Google Earth Engine (GEE) catalog 
(dataset: `COPERNICUS/S1_GRD`). The script filters by:
- Instrument Mode: Interferometric Wide Swath (IW)
- Resolution: High Resolution (10m grid spacing)
- Pass Direction: Descending or Ascending (kept consistent to prevent look-angle distortion)
- Region: A polygon bounding the target farmland coordinates.

The extracted layers are exported as high-resolution spatial GeoTIFF raster grids.

================================================================================
PHASE 2: ADAPTIVE 3X3 LEE SPECKLE FILTERING
================================================================================
Because SAR is a coherent imaging system, the transmitted waves interfere with 
each other constructive and destructively within each pixel. This creates an 
inherent multiplicative "salt-and-pepper" noise called SPECKLE.

To clean this speckle, we implement the 3x3 local adaptive Lee filter inside
`src/sar_processor.py`. The algorithm calculates:
1. Local Mean (μ_L) and local spatial variance (σ2_L) in a 3x3 window:
   `local_mean = scipy.ndimage.uniform_filter(img, (3, 3))`
   `local_var = scipy.ndimage.uniform_filter(img**2, (3, 3)) - local_mean**2`
2. Overall noise variance (σ2_N) across the entire image:
   `overall_var = np.mean(local_var)`
3. The adaptive gain switch (k):
   `k = (local_var - overall_var) / local_var` (bounded between 0 and 1)
4. The output filtered pixel value:
   `filtered = local_mean + k * (img - local_mean)`

*   In homogeneous open soil, spatial variance is low (σ2_L ≈ σ2_N), making k -> 0.
    The pixel is replaced with the local mean μ_L, maximizing smoothing.
*   Near structural boundaries, spatial variance is very high (σ2_L >> σ2_N), 
    making k -> 1. The raw pixel value is preserved, protecting edges from blurring.

================================================================================
PHASE 3: MOISTURE PROXY FORMULATION
================================================================================
Once the VV and VH rasters are filtered, we isolate the average backscatter over
our target fields. Because raw backscatter (dB) is not volumetric soil moisture,
we build a weighted relative wetness index:
`proxy = 0.7 * vv_mean + 0.3 * vh_mean`

*   Co-polarized (VV) backscatter represents surface moisture because waves 
    polarized vertically are highly sensitive to surface dielectric constant variations.
*   Cross-polarized (VH) backscatter represents vegetation interactions because
    leaves and crops rotate the polarization plane (depolarization). 

Integrating both stabilizes the proxy against crop growth noise.

================================================================================
PHASE 4: WATER CLOUD MODEL (WCM) CALIBRATION
================================================================================
To isolate the raw soil wetness from the crop biomass, we apply a physical calibration
model called the Water Cloud Model (WCM). The model represents backscatter as:
σ0 = σ0_veg + τ2 * σ0_soil

Where:
*   σ0_veg = A * VWC * cos(θ)  (Vegetation canopy contribution)
*   τ2 = exp(-2 * B * VWC / cos(θ))  (Two-way canopy attenuation)
*   σ0_soil = C + D * soil_moisture  (Bare soil surface dielectric scattering)

By fitting these parameters (A, B, C, D) using historical data, the script
inverts the WCM equation to subtract canopy backscattering and divide by 
attenuation, outputting a highly calibrated estimation of Volumetric Water Content 
(VWC).
Refer to: `src/sar_calibration.py`

================================================================================
PHASE 5: SPATIOTEMPORAL KALMAN FILTER DATA FUSION
================================================================================
Satellites only scan the area once every 6 to 12 days. Physical ground probes
capture hourly data but are limited to a single spot in the field. 
To achieve daily, field-wide precision, we fuse both sources spatiotemporally 
using a 1-Dimensional Kalman Filter inside `src/sar_correction.py` and `src/sar_integration.py`.

The Kalman Filter runs in two continuous steps:
1. PREDICT STEP (Using the dynamic local decay):
   X_pred = F * X_prev + Q
   *(where F is the daily physical drying/evapotranspiration transition factor,
     and Q is the process noise covariance).*
2. UPDATE/CORRECT STEP (Whenever a satellite pass is available):
   Kalman_Gain = P_pred * H^T * (H * P_pred * H^T + R)^-1
   X_fused = X_pred + Kalman_Gain * (Measurement_SAR - H * X_pred)
   *(where H is the observation matrix, and R is the measurement noise covariance).*

This dynamically fuses the spatial accuracy of the satellite with the temporal
frequency of the physical probes, yielding a continuous, highly accurate, and
completely gap-filled Daily Soil Moisture series.

================================================================================
PHASE 6: DOWNSTREAM INTEGRATION & ML FORECASTING
================================================================================
This gap-filled soil moisture series represents the real-time soil condition.
Every day:
1. The Feature Generator constructs a feature vector containing:
   - Today's rainfall, temperature, and fused soil moisture.
   - 7 rolling lag features of soil moisture: `moisture_lag_1` to `moisture_lag_7`.
   - 7 rolling lag features of weather parameters.
2. The 24-dimensional feature vector is passed to the Random Forest Engine.
3. The model extracts individual predictions from its 100 constituent decision trees
   to calculate point predictions (ensemble mean) and real-time uncertainty 
   (ensemble standard deviation).
4. The uncertainty-aware scheduler adjusts the prediction to trigger proactive,
   crop-specific, and mechanical-pump-optimized irrigation schedules!
